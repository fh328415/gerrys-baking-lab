## Ingredients

| Ingredient                              | Quantity |
|-----------------------------------------|----------|
| Unsalted butter (to be browned)         | 1 cup    |
| **Bread flour** (12.7% protein)         | 2¼ cups  |
| Brown sugar                             | 1 cup    |
| White sugar                             | ½ cup    |
| Large eggs                              | 2        |
| Extra egg yolk                          | 1        |
| Pure vanilla extract                    | 2 tsp    |
| Baking soda                             | 1 tsp    |
| Flaky sea salt (for topping)            | ½ tsp    |
| Fine sea salt (in dough)                | 1 tsp    |
| Dark chocolate chips (60% cacao)        | 2 cups   |

> **Control batch:** standard recipe (room-temp all-purpose butter, all-purpose flour, no cold rest).

## Procedure

1. **Brown butter:** Cooked 1 cup butter in a saucepan over medium heat until golden-brown milk solids appeared and nutty aroma emerged (~7 min). Cooled to room temperature.
2. Whisked browned butter with brown sugar and white sugar until combined.
3. Added 2 whole eggs, 1 extra yolk, and vanilla extract. Whisked vigorously for 90 seconds until mixture was pale and slightly ribbony.
4. Folded in bread flour, baking soda, and fine sea salt until just combined. Dough was noticeably stiffer than all-purpose batches.
5. Folded in dark chocolate chips.
6. **Cold rest:** Covered bowl with plastic wrap. Refrigerated for exactly 48 hours at 4°C.
7. Removed dough from refrigerator. Rested at room temperature for 20 minutes.
8. Preheated oven to 375°F / 190°C.
9. Portioned into 2-tablespoon rounds; spaced 5 cm apart. Topped each round with a pinch of flaky sea salt.
10. Baked for 11 minutes. Edges set and golden; centers appeared underdone (correct).
11. Cooled on baking sheet for 5 minutes, then wire rack for 20 minutes before evaluation.
12. Baked control batch in parallel using standard recipe.

## Observed Results

- **Experimental (combined techniques):** Final spread ~4.0 cm — well-controlled despite dark chocolate base. Deep caramel-amber color. Flaky salt visually prominent. Flavor: the most complex of all sessions — immediate caramel-toffee, then hazelnut and dark chocolate, finishing with a faint brine from the flaky salt. Texture: chewy and elastic interior, defined crisp edge. Cookies remained soft the following day.
- **Control:** Final spread ~4.5 cm. Pale color. Pleasant but simple flavor. Standard texture.
- Unanimous preference for experimental batch among all five evaluators.
- No signs of antagonistic interaction between the three interventions — all effects appeared additive or synergistic.

## Conclusions

Hypothesis **confirmed**. The combined intervention batch is the best result recorded in this lab to date. Key findings:

1. **Synergy confirmed:** Brown butter × bread flour × 48h rest did not cancel each other out. The flavor from brown butter was enhanced (not masked) by the structural strength of bread flour and the cold-rest flavor development.
2. **Spread control:** Bread flour's strong gluten network counteracted the spread tendency introduced by the liquid brown butter, resulting in a balanced, well-shaped cookie.
3. **Flaky salt effect:** The addition of flaky sea salt as a topping amplified the sweetness by contrast (sodium suppresses bitterness perception) and added textural interest.
4. **Shelf life improvement:** Cookies remained noticeably softer after 24 hours than any single-variable batch — likely due to the combined moisture-retention effects of brown sugar and the controlled bake via cold-start dough.

**This protocol is designated as the Baking Lab baseline standard.** Future sessions should test deviations from this optimized baseline rather than starting from scratch.

**Open questions for future sessions:**
