## Ingredients

| Ingredient                        | Quantity |
|-----------------------------------|----------|
| All-purpose flour                 | 2¼ cups  |
| Brown sugar                       | 1 cup    |
| White sugar                       | ½ cup    |
| Unsalted butter, room temperature | 1 cup    |
| Large eggs                        | 2        |
| Pure vanilla extract              | 1 tsp    |
| Baking soda                       | 1 tsp    |
| Fine sea salt                     | 1 tsp    |
| Semi-sweet chocolate chips        | 2 cups   |

> **Control batch:** identical recipe replacing brown + white sugar with 1½ cups white sugar only.

## Procedure

1. Preheated oven to 375°F / 190°C. Lined two baking sheets with parchment paper.
2. Creamed butter with brown sugar and white sugar on medium speed until light and fluffy (~3 min).
3. Added eggs one at a time, beating after each addition; then added vanilla extract.
4. In a separate bowl, whisked together flour, baking soda, and salt.
5. Gradually mixed dry ingredients into wet mixture until just combined — do not overmix.
6. Folded in chocolate chips by hand.
7. Portioned dough into 2-tablespoon rounds, spaced 5 cm apart on baking sheet.
8. Baked for 10 minutes until edges were set; centers should appear slightly underdone.
9. Cooled on baking sheet for 5 minutes, then transferred to wire rack.
10. Prepared and baked control batch following the same procedure.

## Observed Results

- **Experimental (brown sugar):** Final spread ~4.3 cm diameter. Warm golden-amber color. Chewy center with lightly crisp edges. Distinct caramel aroma during baking. Texture after 1 hour: surface yielded softly to pressure.
- **Control (white sugar):** Final spread ~3.9 cm diameter. Pale gold color. Uniformly crisper, noticeably less chew. Neutral aroma. Texture after 1 hour: firmer throughout.
- Experimental batch visibly retained more moisture; cookies remained pliable for longer before hardening.

## Conclusions

Hypothesis **confirmed**. The brown sugar batch produced measurably chewier cookies with a richer flavor profile. Two mechanisms account for this:

1. **Hygroscopicity:** Molasses in brown sugar attracts and retains atmospheric moisture, keeping the crumb soft post-bake.
2. **Maillard precursors:** Molasses contributes amino acids and reducing sugars that enhance browning at lower temperatures, producing the deeper color and caramel notes.
