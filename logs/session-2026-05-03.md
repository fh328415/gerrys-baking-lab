## Ingredients

| Ingredient                              | Quantity |
|-----------------------------------------|----------|
| Unsalted butter (to be browned)         | 1 cup    |
| All-purpose flour                       | 2¼ cups  |
| Brown sugar                             | ¾ cup    |
| White sugar                             | ¾ cup    |
| Large eggs                              | 2        |
| Extra egg yolk                          | 1        |
| Pure vanilla extract                    | 2 tsp    |
| Baking soda                             | 1 tsp    |
| Fine sea salt                           | 1 tsp    |
| Dark chocolate chips (60% cacao)        | 2 cups   |

> **Notes:**
> - Extra egg yolk added to compensate for moisture lost during butter browning (~20% water evaporates).
> - Switched to dark chocolate chips to complement the deeper, nuttier flavor profile.
> **Control batch:** identical recipe with standard melted (not browned) butter, no extra yolk.

## Procedure

1. **Brown the butter:** Melted butter in a light-colored saucepan over medium heat, stirring constantly. Continued cooking until milk solids turned golden-brown and a nutty aroma emerged (~6–8 min). Removed from heat immediately to prevent burning. Poured into a bowl and cooled to room temperature (~30 min).
2. Preheated oven to 375°F / 190°C.
3. Whisked browned butter with brown sugar and white sugar until combined (butter is liquid, so no creaming — just mixing).
4. Added whole eggs, extra yolk, and vanilla extract. Whisked until smooth and slightly ribbony (~2 min).
5. Folded in flour, baking soda, and salt until just combined.
6. Folded in dark chocolate chips.
7. Chilled dough for 30 minutes (to compensate for the liquid butter base).
8. Portioned into 2-tablespoon rounds and baked for 11 minutes.
9. Cooled on baking sheet for 5 minutes. Prepared control batch (melted butter, no extra yolk) in parallel.

## Observed Results

- **Experimental (browned butter):** Deep amber color — noticeably darker than any previous batch. Intense nutty, toffee aroma during baking. Final spread ~4.0 cm. Flavor: rich, complex — hazelnut and caramel notes prominent. Texture: slightly drier and denser than brown-sugar batches, but still pleasant chew.
- **Control (melted butter):** Pale golden color. Mild aroma. Flavor: buttery but less nuanced. Texture: softer and slightly greasier.
- The browned butter batch held its shape better, likely due to chilling step and reduced water content.

## Conclusions

Hypothesis **confirmed**. Brown butter produced the most flavorful batch to date. Key findings:

1. **Maillard reaction in the pan:** Browning butter causes the milk solids (proteins + lactose) to undergo Maillard browning and caramelization, generating hundreds of new aromatic compounds — primarily diacetyl, furanones, and pyrazines.
2. **Water loss effect:** Evaporating ~20% of the butter's water concentrates the fat, which affects dough consistency (less spread, slightly drier crumb). The extra egg yolk partially compensated.
3. **Flavor synergy:** Dark chocolate chips amplified the nutty and bitter notes rather than clashing with them.

**Recommended next step:** Combine brown butter with bread flour (high-protein flour) in a single batch to test for compounding textural and flavor improvements.
