## Ingredients

| Ingredient                        | Quantity |
|-----------------------------------|----------|
| All-purpose flour                 | 2¼ cups  |
| Brown sugar                       | ¾ cup    |
| White sugar                       | ¾ cup    |
| Unsalted butter, room temperature | 1 cup    |
| Large eggs                        | 2        |
| Pure vanilla extract              | 2 tsp    |
| Baking soda                       | 1 tsp    |
| Fine sea salt                     | 1 tsp    |
| Semi-sweet chocolate chips        | 2 cups   |

> **Control batch:** same dough baked immediately after mixing (0-hour rest).

## Procedure

1. Prepared full dough batch following the standard protocol (see session 2026-04-10, steps 1–6).
2. Divided dough into two equal portions.
3. **Experimental portion:** Wrapped tightly in plastic wrap, refrigerated for exactly 48 hours at 4°C.
4. **Control portion:** Portioned immediately and baked at 375°F / 190°C for 10 minutes.
5. After 48-hour rest, removed experimental dough from refrigerator. Allowed to sit at room temperature for 15 minutes before portioning.
6. Portioned experimental dough into 2-tablespoon rounds, spaced 5 cm apart.
7. Baked experimental batch at 375°F / 190°C for 11 minutes (1 min longer due to colder starting temperature).
8. Cooled both batches on wire rack for 30 minutes before taste evaluation.

## Observed Results

- **Experimental (48h rest):** Noticeably darker golden-brown color. Dough was stiffer and held its shape better — minimal spread (~3.8 cm). Flavor: complex toffee and butterscotch notes, mild nuttiness, less sweet on first bite. Texture: slightly firmer edge, chewy interior.
- **Control (0h rest):** Lighter color, more spread (~4.4 cm). Flavor: straightforward sweet/buttery, less depth. Texture: softer and more uniform.
- Smell during baking was markedly more intense in the experimental batch.

## Conclusions

Hypothesis **confirmed**. The 48-hour cold rest produced cookies with noticeably greater flavor complexity. Proposed mechanisms:

1. **Enzymatic activity:** Amylase enzymes in flour break down some starch into simpler sugars during the rest period, increasing substrate availability for Maillard reactions.
2. **Moisture redistribution:** Extended rest allows water to distribute more evenly through the dough, resulting in more uniform gluten development and controlled spread.
3. **Flavor concentration:** Partial evaporation of surface moisture from the unwrapped dough (if applicable) can concentrate aromatic compounds.

**Open question:** Does a 72-hour rest yield further improvement or diminishing returns? Proposed follow-up session to test 24h vs 48h vs 72h as a three-condition experiment.
