## Ingredients

| Ingredient                        | Quantity    |
|-----------------------------------|-------------|
| All-purpose flour                 | 2 cups      |
| Cornstarch                        | 2 tbsp      |
| Brown sugar                       | ¾ cup       |
| White sugar                       | ¾ cup       |
| Unsalted butter, room temperature | 1 cup       |
| Large eggs                        | 2           |
| Pure vanilla extract              | 1 tsp       |
| Baking soda                       | 1 tsp       |
| Fine sea salt                     | 1 tsp       |
| Semi-sweet chocolate chips        | 2 cups      |

> **Note:** Temperature reduced to 350°F vs previous sessions to prevent over-browning, compensated by 2 extra minutes bake time.
> **Control batch:** standard recipe with 2¼ cups all-purpose flour, no cornstarch.

## Procedure

1. Preheated oven to 350°F / 175°C. Lined two baking sheets with parchment paper.
2. In a bowl, whisked together all-purpose flour, cornstarch, baking soda, and salt. Set aside.
3. Creamed butter with brown sugar and white sugar until light and fluffy (~3 min).
4. Added eggs one at a time, then vanilla extract.
5. Gradually incorporated dry mixture into wet. Mixed until just combined.
6. Folded in chocolate chips.
7. Portioned dough into 2-tablespoon rounds, spaced 5 cm apart.
8. Baked for 12 minutes. Centers appeared very soft — monitored closely to avoid over-baking.
9. Cooled on baking sheet for 8 minutes before transferring (more fragile than previous batches).
10. Prepared and baked control batch at the same temperature and time.

## Observed Results

- **Experimental (cornstarch):** Cookies spread significantly — final diameter ~5.1 cm, noticeably wider than previous sessions. Color was even and pale golden. Texture: exceptionally soft and pillowy center. Almost cake-like crumb. Edges barely crisped.
- **Control:** Final diameter ~4.2 cm. Standard texture — slightly crisp edge, moderate chew.
- The cornstarch batch was fragile while warm; two cookies broke during transfer.
- After 2 hours of cooling, experimental cookies remained noticeably softer than control.

## Conclusions

Hypothesis **partially confirmed**. Adding cornstarch did produce a softer, more tender crumb, consistent with the gluten-dilution mechanism:

- **Mechanism:** Cornstarch has no gluten-forming proteins. Replacing ~10% of the flour volume with cornstarch weakens the overall protein network, producing a finer, more tender texture.
- **Unintended side effect:** Reduced structural integrity caused excessive spread and made cookies difficult to handle while warm.

**Recommendation:** Reduce spread in the next trial by (a) adding 1 extra tablespoon of flour to partially compensate for structural loss, and/or (b) chilling dough for 30 minutes before baking. The soft crumb outcome is desirable; the spread needs to be controlled.
